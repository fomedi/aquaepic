About this study

Structured expert elicitation for SRS management in Scotland

Piscirickettsiosis (SRS) is one of the most significant infectious constraints on Scottish Atlantic salmon farming. This project captures structured expert knowledge to prioritise control measures under real production conditions.

Despite growing epidemiological evidence, there is limited structured data on how disease control measures are comparatively prioritised by field experts under Scottish production conditions. This study addresses that gap using a rigorous, transparent methodology.

Using a modified Delphi approach, a panel of 8–12 Scottish salmon fish health specialists rates each shortlisted measure on two independent dimensions: perceived effectiveness (0–4 scale) and feasibility of implementation (0–4 scale). This produces a 2×2 priority matrix — Act Now / Enable / Reconsider / Deprioritise — that is directly usable by veterinarians, farm managers, and policymakers.

The study is funded by the Animal Health and Welfare Cluster Pump-Priming Programme at the Royal (Dick) School of Veterinary Studies, University of Edinburgh, and is connected to the AquaEPIC initiative — a proposed science–policy–industry interface for aquaculture health governance in Scotland.

Methodology

Four-phase study design

The study progresses from collaborative list co-design with industry representatives through expert screening, dual-score Delphi ranking, and a final in-person synthesis at Easter Bush.

01
In progress
List building & instrument design
May 2026 · Weeks 1–3
Two industry representatives with direct SRS experience review, expand, and refine a master list of ~100 candidate control measures. They also pilot the survey instrument before wider distribution.
02
June 2026
Screening ballot
Weeks 4–5 · Opens 2 June
The full expert panel votes Keep or Remove for each of the ~100 measures. Simple majority rule. Measures with ≥50% Keep votes proceed to Delphi ranking, shortlisting ≤35 measures.
03
June–July 2026
Delphi ranking
Weeks 6–8 · Two rounds
Experts rate each shortlisted measure on Effectiveness (0–4) and Feasibility (0–4) independently. Round 2 feeds back anonymised group results. Probabilistic uncertainty elicited for top measures.
04
October 2026
Easter Bush synthesis
In-person workshop · Edinburgh
The AquaEPIC science–policy–industry workshop at Easter Bush Campus serves as a Round 3 sense-check. Contested measures are deliberated face-to-face before final manuscript revision.

Participant Information Sheet

Version 1 · May 2026

Study title: Expert elicitation to prioritise risk and protective factors for bacterial diseases in Scottish salmon aquaculture.

What is the purpose of the study? This study aims to identify and prioritise key risk and protective factors influencing bacterial disease occurrence, timing, and severity in Scottish salmon aquaculture, using a structured expert elicitation (Delphi) approach.

Why have I been invited? You have been invited because of your professional expertise in fish health, aquaculture, or related fields. Your experience is valuable in helping to identify and prioritise factors influencing disease dynamics.

What will participation involve?

  • Completing an online screening ballot and Delphi questionnaire (Round 1) — approximately 20–30 minutes
  • Taking part in a structured discussion (online or in person)
  • Completing a second questionnaire (Round 2) — approximately 20–30 minutes
  • Optional attendance at a final workshop at the University of Edinburgh to review and discuss aggregated results

Will my participation be confidential? Yes. All responses will be anonymised prior to analysis and reporting. However, please note that during group discussions and the optional workshop, participants may be known to each other. While anonymity cannot be guaranteed in these settings, no individual responses will be attributed in any outputs.

Will the study be recorded? No. No audio or video recordings will be made. Notes may be taken in anonymised form.

Do I have to take part? No. Participation is entirely voluntary. You are free to withdraw at any time without giving a reason and without any consequences.

What will happen to my data?

  • Limited personal data (name and email address) will be collected for communication purposes only
  • Research data will be anonymised and analysed in aggregate form
  • Data will be stored securely on University of Edinburgh systems
  • Data will be retained for up to 5 years and then securely deleted
  • Fully anonymised results may be used in academic publications and reports

What are the risks? This study is considered low risk. The main consideration is that, during group discussions, participants may be identifiable to each other. This will be managed by ensuring that no individual responses are reported.

Will I receive the results? Yes. A summary of the findings will be shared with participants via email upon completion of the study.

Expert portal

Phase 2: Screening ballot

Invited experts vote to keep or remove each of the ~100 candidate measures, shortlisting those most relevant to Scottish production conditions for the Delphi ranking rounds.

How the ballot works

You will see all candidate measures grouped by thematic section and tagged with their APDR governance stage (Anticipate / Prevent / Detect / Respond). For each measure, you vote Keep or Remove based on relevance to Scottish salmon farming.

Measures retained by ≥50% of respondents will proceed to the Delphi ranking rounds, where you will rate each on Effectiveness and Feasibility on a 0–4 scale.

  1. 1You receive a unique access token by email from the study team
  2. 2Complete the ballot — estimated 20–30 minutes at your own pace
  3. 3Anonymised screening results shared with all panellists before Round 1
  4. 4Delphi Round 1 opens 16 June — dual scoring (Effectiveness + Feasibility)
